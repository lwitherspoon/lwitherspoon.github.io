My Global Framework

To others, global economic development often covers a specific set of topics related to ending poverty; however, my approach to global economic development extends beyond ending poverty and focuses more on the geopolitical reasons for which poverty and economic instability exist in certain regions. In viewing the poverty dilemma through a political set of lenses, my approach turns the spotlight on how key political actors such as national and local governments and NGOs working in collaboration with those governments influence the economic sphere. As realists argue (with which I agree), states will seek relative power gains in situations of anarchy (including economic anarchy). The global political power struggle often, if not always, influences economic cooperation and interaction among states - it is this relationship that drives my academic body of work.
